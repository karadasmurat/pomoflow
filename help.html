What PomoFlow is

Most productivity tools measure one thing: whether you finished. PomoFlow measures something different — whether you showed up.

Instead of tracking task completion, it tracks the effort you invest across the areas of work, study, and life that matter to you. Every session you complete is proof of progress, regardless of where the finish line sits.

The result is a different relationship with your time. Not guilt when things aren't done, but clarity about where your energy is actually going — and whether it reflects what you care about.

PomoFlow is built around a simple loop you repeat daily: Define what deserves your attention. Commit to where a block of time is going before you start. Focus by showing up fully for each session. Reflect on how your effort is flowing across your areas.

01

Your first session

The fastest way to understand PomoFlow is to run a session.

1
Create a Focus Area. Open the Focus Areas panel (G) and add your first area. Give it a name and a color.
2
Set your intent. Tap What are you focusing on? and select your area. This is where busy becomes purposeful — you're naming, before the work begins, where this time is going.
3
Start the timer. Press Space or tap Start. A 25-minute focus session begins. Your only job is to be present for it.
4
Take your break. When the session ends, the timer holds at 0:00. PomoFlow waits for you — it won't rush you. Press Space when you're ready.
5
Repeat. After four focus sessions, a longer break is triggered. That's one full Pomodoro cycle.

When you're done, your time is logged against your Focus Area and appears in your effort history.

02

The timer

Modes

Mode Default Purpose
Focus 25 minutes Deep work dedicated to a Focus Area
Short Break 5 minutes A brief pause to reset between sessions
Long Break 15 minutes Extended rest after every 4 focus sessions

Switch modes manually at any time using 1, 2, or 3.

Accuracy

The timer uses wall-clock accuracy — it tracks your session's target end-time rather than counting down in the foreground. Sessions stay precise even if your browser tab moves to the background or your device sleeps briefly.

Adjusting durations

Open the Orbit menu (the timer icon, also known as the Watch Crown) to set durations in preset increments between 10 and 60 minutes. Use + and − for fine-tuning. When active, the timer ring displays Guiding Content to help you make adjustments. Changes take effect at the start of your next session.

03

Focus Areas

Focus Areas are the categories you invest time in. They're not just labels — they're a declaration of what you're choosing to show up for.

Creating and editing areas

Open the Focus Areas panel with G. Add an area, give it a name, choose a color and an icon. Most people find that four to six areas covers everything that genuinely matters.

Attributing sessions

Before each session, select the area you're working in. If you forget, you can edit the attribution from the Activity Log after the fact.

XP and levels

Every minute of focused time earns XP. Your rank advances as you build consistency — from Novice upward through higher tiers. It's a reflection of investment over time, not a productivity score.

04

The Focus Planner

The Focus Planner is where you move from reactive sessions to intentional weeks. Open it with P.

It's a weekly calendar where you place commitment blocks — planned effort against your Focus Areas — and deadline markers — real-world moments you're working toward. Completed sessions automatically appear as a second layer.

Commitment blocks

A commitment block is an intention: I will give this Focus Area N sessions on this day.

On desktop, drag a Focus Area card from the sidebar and drop it onto the day and time you want. A popover appears for session count and an optional label. Drag the bottom edge to adjust after placement.

On mobile, tap any day column to open the day drawer, then tap an empty slot or +. Set a start time and use the stepper to set session count. The end time updates live.

Block duration is calculated from your session settings — so it always reflects how long that number of sessions will actually take.

Deadline markers

A deadline marker is an event in the world — a submission, a presentation, a moment you're planning toward. Add one from the + button in the header and optionally link it to relevant Focus Areas.

Deadline markers never turn red as the date approaches. The calendar is not here to generate urgency — it's here to give you clarity.

Planned vs. actual

The calendar shows your commitment blocks (planned) and completed session blocks (actual) simultaneously. Use the filter to toggle between views.

The gap between planned and actual is information, not failure. Drift is normal. The value is in being able to see it clearly.
05

Seeing your progress

The Dashboard

The Dashboard shows a visual breakdown across Focus Areas, total focus time, session count, your current day streak, and a full Activity Log. You can edit session durations or delete entries from the log if needed.

Logical day rollover

PomoFlow uses a 4:00 AM rollover for daily calculations. Sessions completed before 4 AM are attributed to the previous calendar date, so late-night work lands in the right day's totals.

06

Your profile

Persona

Your chosen Persona appears as an avatar in the header, alongside your Rank and XP, and stays visible across the app. It's not a fixed identity — it's a reflection of how you're showing up right now.

Browse and select from the Persona Gallery in your Profile. Change it whenever your mood shifts. There's no right answer and no schedule.

Rank and XP

Your Rank and XP accumulate automatically as you log focused time, displayed in the header next to your Persona. They're a record of investment over time — not a performance target.

Customization

Adjust focus, short break, and long break durations in Settings. Toggle auto-start if you prefer the timer to advance automatically between phases.

Privacy and data

All data is stored locally in your browser — nothing is sent to a server. Use the Export and Import tools in Settings → Data to back up or migrate your history.

Clearing your browser's site data will reset your PomoFlow history. Export regularly if your history matters to you.

Technical requirements

Browser: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge (current versions).

Notifications: Enable browser notifications in Settings to receive alerts when sessions end.

Storage: Local storage must be enabled for PomoFlow to save your data.
