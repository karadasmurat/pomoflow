A different way to measure progress

Traditional task managers deal in binary outcomes: done or not done. That framing is useful for checklists. It's not useful for hard problems, creative work, or anything that requires sustained thinking over days.

When you frame work as a time investment rather than a deliverable, something shifts. You stop dreading the deadline and start directing your energy. You can't always control when something is finished — but you can always control whether you showed up for it.

PomoFlow is built around that shift. Every focus session is attributed to a specific area of work, so over time you don't just have a timer history — you have a honest record of where your energy actually went.

"Focus for an hour, and you are 50% done with your intent. That is progress."

How it works

Four steps. A loop you repeat daily.

1
Define
Name your focus areas

Add the areas of work, study, or life that deserve your attention. Give each a color. These become the lens through which all your effort is tracked.

2
Commit
Set your intent before you start

Before each session, choose the focus area you're investing in. This small act of naming your intent — rather than just starting a timer — is where the mindset shift begins.

3
Focus
Work. Rest. Repeat.

The timer guides you through focused work blocks and restorative breaks. Your only job is to be present for the session you committed to.

4
Reflect
See where your energy went

Review your effort breakdown across focus areas. Not to judge — to understand. Is your time flowing toward what actually matters to you? Awareness is the first step to alignment.
