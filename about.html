About & How to Use

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that uses a timer to break work into focused intervals, separated by short breaks. This trains your brain to focus and helps prevent burnout.

  1. Choose a task you want to work on.
  2. Set the timer for 25 minutes and start working without interruption.
  3. When the timer rings, take a 5-minute short break.
  4. After four focus sessions, take a longer 15-30 minute break.

The PomoFlow Lifecycle

PomoFlow automates this cycle for you. The visual Cycle Tracker above the timer shows your progress. As you complete each session, the corresponding dot on the tracker will fill in.

Key Features

Task Management

Click "Open Tasks" or press T to manage your to-do list. Clicking the play button next to a task links it to your focus session for better tracking.

Task Colors

Categorize your work by assigning one of six colors to your tasks. This helps you visually distinguish between different projects or types of work in your history.

Customizable Durations

Go to Settings (the gear icon) to change the length of focus, short break, and long break periods to match your personal workflow.

Session History

The app automatically logs all completed focus sessions. You can review your productivity by day, week, or see your entire history at a glance.

Keyboard Shortcuts

SpacebarStart or Pause the current timer. TToggle the task drawer open or closed. RReset the timer for the current mode. NSkip to the next session in the cycle. 1Switch to Focus mode. 2Switch to Short Break mode. 3Switch to Long Break mode.
